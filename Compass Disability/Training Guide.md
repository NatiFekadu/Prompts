## Acronyms / Definitions

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **MRF** | Medical Report Form |
| **MRFCA** | Mental Residual Function Capacity Assessment |
| **DLI** | Date Last Insured — date SSDI insurance expires |
| **ABD** | Aged/Blind/Disabled program (DSHS cash program, ~$197/mo). Requires SSI application; if SSI approved, SSI pays ABD back. SSDI does not. |
| **AOD** | Alleged Onset Date. Key factors: when they stopped working full-time, whether conditions caused the stop, when consistent treatment began. |
| **Title 2 / SSDI / DIB / SSD** | Same program. 5-month waiting period before benefits begin. |
| **Title 16 / SSI** | Needs-based program. |
| **SGA** | Substantial Gainful Activity. 2025 limit: $1,620/month. |
| **OTP** | Out of Town Packet |
| **DDDS / DDS** | Department of Disability Determination Services |
| **DAC** | Disabled Adult Child |
| **POMS** | Programs Operation Manual System — SSA's rulebook |
| **TAFI** | Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (housing) |
| **HEN** | Housing and Essential Needs (state program, $500–$700/mo) |
| **UWA** | Unsuccessful Work Attempt — documented work stop caused by condition |
| **PPD** | Partial Permanent Disability — lump-sum settlement from a workplace injury |
| **L&I** | Labor and Industry — state program similar to SS; pays only the greater of the two |
| **Res judicata** | A matter finally adjudicated; no further appeals possible |
| **Chavez rule** | Subsequent decisions presume non-disability unless rebutted (POMS DI 52755.010) |
| **De Novo hearing** | A brand-new ALJ hearing, not bound by prior DDS/FRO findings |
| **SDM** | Single Decision Maker — DDS examiners who can issue initial determinations without medical consultant signature (pilot in 20 DDSs) |

## Lead Intake Script (used by staff)

### Initial contact

> "Hi, this is ____ calling from Compass Disability. I received the request for help with Social Security Disability that you filled out online. Based on what you put here, you are dealing with _______. Is that right?"

### Voicemail

> "Hi ___, this is ____. I am responding to the form you filled out online requesting help with Social Security disability. It takes about 5 minutes to see if you are eligible to apply. You can reach me at 801-810-7734, and I will also send a text message in case that is a better way to reach you. Thanks!"

### Qualifying questions

1. Have you ever applied for Social Security in the past? (If yes: how long ago? Denial letter? Reason? If hearing level: when, where, judge, T2/SSI, ABD, MRF)
2. Are you currently seeing doctors about your conditions? How often?
3. Work history — are you currently working? If no, when last? If yes: sick time / able to do job / part- or full-time / earnings more or less than $1,000/mo? How long at last job? Prior jobs? Out of the last 10 years, how many did you not work — more or less than half? Self-employed? SSA taxes withheld?
4. How are you currently paying for food and housing? Workers comp, PPD, unemployment, tribal stipend? Other income (wages, alimony, child support, settlements)?
5. Married? Spouse income?
6. Checking/savings balance?
7. Vehicles (one + primary home ignored; additional vehicles count as assets)?
8. Other assets — property, retirement, stocks, bonds, anything sellable for more than $1,000?

### Substance abuse note
SSA can deny claims where substance abuse is causing or worsening conditions. Long periods of sobriety help.

### Fee explanation (verbatim)

> "It is a standard fee agreement that is set by and administered by Social Security. They pay us directly out of what is called your 'back pay.' If you get approved, in some cases SSA will determine that they owe you past-due benefits — that's back pay. 75% goes to you and we get 25%. The monthly benefit from then on is 100% yours."

## Dire Need / Expedited / Critical Cases

SSA expedites claims for:

- **Dire financial need**: food insecurity, lack of medical care, unsafe shelter (homeless, facing eviction/foreclosure, uninhabitable home/no utilities)
- **Safety concerns**: suicidal/homicidal tendencies, threats to self or others
- **Terminal illness**
- **Military service**: wounded warriors, active-duty injuries
- **Compassionate allowance**: certain qualifying medical conditions

## Appeals

If client can't be reached after 45 days post-denial, appeal without them to keep the case moving.

### General timing

- 60 days from denial to appeal. Compass Disability target: 30 days.
- Denial naming: `Initial Denial (Medical/Tech) – T2/T16 – [date]` or `Recon Denied (Medical) – T2/T16 – [date]`
- Add MyCase workflow to track the deadline (critical).
- Attempt to reach client ≥3 times: week 1, week 3, then 2–3 more tries in week 4.
- If appealing without speaking to client, sticky note it: `submitted appeal without speaking to client. [Initials] [Date].`
- Always do a medical update, especially for DSHS. Re-request records listed as "(date) – Present." Send contact letter if not already sent.

### Questions to ask for the appeal

1. Current mailing/physical address
2. Phone number confirmation + backup
3. Have they worked? How much? (Explain SGA.)
4. Have conditions worsened? How and when?
5. (Initial denials only) Check facilities listed on the denial — if client has been treated there since DDS received records, list them in the appeal.

### During the internet appeal

- Save the **Re-entry Number** in MyCase notes.
- Reason for Appeal: `Claimant is still unable to work at SGA levels` (adults) or `Claimant is still disabled` (children).
- Submit dated SSA-827 and any outstanding medical records with the appeal (note in MyCase history).
- Upload signed Manner of Appearance form.
- Save PDF of submitted records to client's Google Drive appeal folder; save receipt in MyCase history.

### After the appeal

- Add completion step to Claims section in MyCase.
- If unable to appeal online and no one else has appealed, file a paper appeal.
  - **Paper Reconsideration**: Cover Letter, SSA-827, 3441, SSA-561, online error message.
  - **Paper Hearing**: Cover Letter, SSA-827, 3441, HA-501, online error message.

### Good Cause appeals

File the appeal first; provide the good-cause letter after. Criteria include:

- Handwritten client letter
- Misleading SSA actions / notices not received / homelessness
- Physical, mental, educational, or linguistic limitations
- Hospitalization, death, or serious family illness
- Good-faith submission to the wrong agency

Use citation: `CFR 404.911. Good cause for missing the deadline to request review.` Example phrasing: `Consistent with CFR 404.911(b)(8), we request that the claim be reopened...`

Submit paper REQ FOR RECON 561 + 3441 with the handwritten letter.

### Appeal Link
Found in Employee Documents → manila folder on Google Drive.

### Aged Claim Checks
MyCase → Reports → Social Security → Reports by Level with Totals. Review all cases past their level timeframe. Check history before calling SSA. Escalate findings to Ben or Tim.

## Hearings

### Request for Hearing (RFH) steps

1. Complete the RFH appeal.
2. Summary of request (1–2 weeks after request). Clients keep for records. No medical facilities listed at this level.
3. Send signed Manner of Appearance form. Default to telephone hearings unless the client explicitly requested in-person and Ben cleared it.
4. Notice of Exhibited Files — electronic file is ready to view in ERE. Client does not need to complete the Recent TX / Medications / Work Background / 827 packet (we handle as their representative).
5. Always confirm client contact info.

### Medical Updates

- Calendar alert appears 1 month before the hearing.
- Schedule a 30-min medical update with the client. Log in to ERE, pull the prior sources, and ask:
  - More treatment at these clinics / doctors?
  - Any new sources?
  - What conditions are stopping them from working? Which clinics treat those?
  - Work history + current (full-/part-time, after AOD, pay, hours).
- T2 claim: check DLI. If in the past, focus on records before that date.
- Verify addresses / locations / dates of service. Add new ones.
- Tell client to send any new records; otherwise we'll request them.
- If few sources and no new treatment → email Ben early.
- Add notes in MyCase; track missing sources.
- MRFs — check if any received; ask for more willing doctors.
- Schedule PH consultation with Ben: 4 PM and 5 PM a day or two before the hearing. (More than 2 hearings in a week → schedule a week before.)
- Check off "medical update" task. Email Kaylee when complete.
- Follow up on records in ~1 week. Request records if client can't get them by 2 weeks before hearing.
- 5-day letter created 5–10 business days before hearing. List missing sources + pending MRFs.

### 5-Day Letter / Electronic File

- Calendar reminder 7 business days before the hearing. Must be submitted **5 business days** before (watch federal holidays).
- Download the Exhibit File (EF): Exhibit list → Select All → Download Selected to PDF.
- If EF <~200 pages or >~1,500 pages of MER → email Ben.

### Mental Health Cases

Harder to approve due to subjectivity. Recommend exams: MOCA, CAFAS, DLA-20, mGAF, WHODAS. MRFCAs and exams reduce subjectivity.

### Postponement Requests

Good cause per 20 CFR 404.936(f):
- Serious physical/mental condition, incapacitating injury, or family death
- Severe weather preventing travel
- Other factors: need time to find representation; new rep (<30 days); rep has prior court/hearing commitment; witness unavailable; no transportation; unrepresented claimant with physical/mental/educational/linguistic limitations

### Hearing Denials
If claimant no longer wishes to pursue, note in MyCase and let it lapse.

## Partially Favorable Decisions

Disability date differs from claimed date. SSI within date of application = fully favorable. Sometimes SSI approved but SSDI denied for expired DLI without enough pre-DLI evidence.

Client options:
- **Appeal**: could be upgraded to fully favorable, left as PF, or reversed to fully denied.
- **No appeal**: leave as is; lower risk.

If appeal considered: request CD file from SSA FO (hearings already have EF). Scan CD proof (postmarks matter). Notify Ben for review. Present Ben's recommendation to the client.

## 821 / 820 / Work Attempts

**Purpose**: show a closed period, SGA status, or UWA after AOD.

- Pull client docs (page 29). Q1 should always be Yes.
- Fill employer info. Mark "no paystubs" if none available — SSA usually accepts.
- Enter monthly income for each month being proven.
- Repeat for additional employers.
- **UWA**: starts first month over SGA, ends after 30 days under SGA. Example: 5 months over SGA + 1 under = UWA. Months over SGA get no benefits.
- **Closed period**: requires 12 months disability, all under SGA.
- 820 = same, but for self-employment (not W-2).

## Dealing with Upset Clients

**1. Empathize.**
- "I'm sorry to hear that." / "That's awful." / "I hear you."
- Validate externally without internalizing.

**2. Inform.**
- Summarize what's been done.
- Explain the process.
- Name what went wrong.

**3. Take action.**
- Tell them what WE will do.
- Give them a concrete way to help: get more treatment, find supportive doctors, request MRFs, get their own records.

## Assets (SSI-only)

SSI asset limit: $2k individual / $3k married. T2 has no asset limit.

- One car + one house = ignored.
- Additional vehicles/properties/valuables count. If 2+ cars, SSA typically uses the more expensive one (check equity).

**Do not advise to sell or gift.**

- **Selling**: proceeds still count as an asset. Must (1) sell and (2) spend before eligibility.
- **Gifting**: 36-month disqualification period.

## Client Passes Away

- Cause of death related to their conditions? Benefits may still be recoverable — need death certificate.
- Beneficiary required for payout. Hierarchy:
  1. Surviving spouse who lived in the same household at death, or was entitled to a monthly benefit on the same record for month of death (common-law in some states).
  2. Children entitled to a benefit on the same record for month of death.
  3. Parents entitled to a benefit on the same record for month of death.
  4. Surviving spouse (not under #1).
  5. Children (not under #2).
  6. Parents (not under #3).
  7. Legal representative of the estate.
