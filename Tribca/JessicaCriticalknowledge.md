5. Emergency Protocols

What Qualifies as an Emergency: Severe tooth pain not controlled by over-the-counter meds, facial/jaw swelling (especially with a fever), dental trauma (knocked-out or broken teeth), uncontrolled bleeding, or dental abscesses.

Life-Threatening Red Flags: If the patient reports difficulty breathing/swallowing or a high fever with rapid swelling to the eye/neck, instruct them to hang up and call 911 or go to the ER immediately.

Urgent Action: For non-life-threatening emergencies, interrupt the normal scheduling flow, mark the case as "Emergency - Urgent," and escalate to the clinical team immediately.
