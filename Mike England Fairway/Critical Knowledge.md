## Reverse Mortgage (HECM) Basics

**What it is:** A Home Equity Conversion Mortgage lets homeowners 62 and older convert a portion of their home equity into cash without selling the home or making monthly mortgage payments.

**Borrower eligibility (general):**
- At least 62 years old (youngest borrower).
- Property must be the borrower's primary residence.
- Sufficient equity in the home.
- Borrower must complete HUD-approved counseling.
- Borrower must demonstrate ability and willingness to keep up with property taxes, homeowners insurance, and home maintenance.

**Eligible property types (general):**
- Single-family homes.
- 2-4 unit homes where one unit is owner-occupied.
- HUD-approved condominiums.
- Manufactured homes meeting FHA requirements.

**Key benefits:**
- No monthly mortgage payments required (borrower still owes property taxes, homeowners insurance, and maintenance).
- Growing line of credit feature on unused funds.
- Cash can be taken as lump sum, monthly payments, line of credit, or a combination.
- Borrower retains title.
- Loan is non-recourse -- borrower or heirs never owe more than the home's value when the loan becomes due.
- Borrower can sell the home anytime.
- Repayment deferred until the last borrower permanently leaves the home (death, move-out, or sale).

**Important responsibilities:**
- Borrower must continue paying property taxes.
- Borrower must continue paying homeowners insurance.
- Borrower must maintain the home in good condition.
- Failure to meet these obligations could trigger loan default.

**Not suitable for everyone:** A reverse mortgage is one tool of many. Mike's approach is education-first, no pressure.
