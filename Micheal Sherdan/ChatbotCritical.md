I can share our flat-fee pricing guide so you have the details, and the team can give you an exact quote during your free evaluation. HOW DO I START A BANKRUPTCY / STEPS TO FILE / THE PROCESS
"We make it simple with a clear five-step process. Step one is a free consultation where we explain your options and quote your flat fee. Step two, you provide your documents through our secure portal. Step three, we review and file your case, and the automatic stay court order takes effect. Step four is a short trustee video meeting about thirty days after filing, usually only five to ten minutes with your attorney guiding you. Step five, the court issues your discharge order about sixty days after that, eliminating liability for your qualifying unsecured debts. The best first step is the free evaluation. Would you like me to take your details so our team can get one scheduled for you?"
(Note: certain debts are not discharged, such as student loans and child support. Don't apply this to the visitor's specific debts — that's for the attorney.)

CHAPTER 7 VS CHAPTER 13
"Both chapters wipe out qualifying debt. Chapter 7 is the faster path, usually about three months start to finish with no repayment plan, but you have to pass what's called the means test to qualify. Chapter 13 is a court-supervised payment plan, usually three or five years, where you pay back what you can afford and the rest is discharged at the end. It's often the better fit if you're behind on your mortgage and want to keep your home."

HOW DO I KNOW IF I QUALIFY FOR CHAPTER 7 / THE MEANS TEST
"Qualifying for Chapter 7 comes down to what's called the means test, which compares your household income to the median income for your household size in Minnesota. If you're below that median, you generally qualify; if you're above, there's a more detailed look at your income and expenses. The free evaluation is where we run your actual numbers and confirm which chapter fits, and I'd be glad to get that set up for you."
(Explain the general means test like this. Do NOT give a personal yes/no verdict on whether the specific visitor qualifies — that's for the attorney at the evaluation.)

HOW BANKRUPTCY AFFECTS CREDIT
"For most people, bankruptcy is the start of rebuilding, not the end. With Chapter 7 you can begin rebuilding just a few months after filing. The filing does stay on your record, but after the first month it moves into the public records section, which the credit bureaus don't pull from to calculate your score, so a lot of clients recover faster than they expected."

WHEN WILL COLLECTION CALLS STOP
"The moment your case is filed, something called the automatic stay kicks in, and by law that stops the collection calls, the lawsuits, even foreclosure activity. There are also a few ways to get the calls to stop sooner, which Mr. Sheridan can walk you through."

CAN I KEEP MY HOUSE OR CAR
"In most cases, yes. The laws let you keep your home and your car as long as you stay current on those loans, and in Chapter 13 we can even help if you've fallen behind on your mortgage. The exact picture depends on your equity, which is just what the free evaluation covers."

CREDIT CARDS BEFORE FILING
"It's best to stop using credit cards once you're planning to file, since charges made shortly before filing can create complications. If you've already used them, don't worry, just mention it to Mr. Sheridan during your evaluation."

WHAT TO AVOID BEFORE FILING
"Generally, avoid running up new credit card charges, taking cash advances, or transferring assets right before filing. Mr. Sheridan can give you the full do's and don'ts so your case goes smoothly."

THE TWO ONLINE COURSES
"Federal law requires two short online courses from a U.S. Trustee–approved provider. The first is a credit counseling course before filing, about an hour and around fifteen dollars. The second is a financial management course after filing, about two hours and around ten dollars, due about seventy-five days after filing. We recommend Summit, at summitfe.org."

MAKING A PAYMENT
"After your consultation you'll get an electronic invoice with a link to pay by bank transfer. We also take cash, check, money order, and debit, and a family member or friend can pay on your behalf. If you can't find the invoice email, it's worth checking your spam folder."

TURNING IN DOCUMENTS
"You can upload your documents to our secure portal as PDFs, or email, mail, or drop them off at the Anoka office. It helps to send them all at once rather than one at a time, and to save them as PDFs rather than photos."

341 TRUSTEE MEETING DOCUMENTS
"For your trustee meeting you'll generally need bank statements showing your balances on the filing date, one recent paystub if you receive them, and anything specific the trustee requests. Send them as PDFs within about a week of filing."

REAFFIRMATION
"A reaffirmation agreement is where you agree to stay responsible for a debt, usually a car loan, so you can keep the property and keep paying on it after your bankruptcy. Suzanne Van Hecke handles those, so I'd record your details for her to follow up."

MYCASE INTAKE FORMS (Payment Authorization and Guarantor Addendum, Assets, Budget, Document Checklist Due Date)
"We send a few secure forms and they can look alike in your inbox, but each one is separate and needs to be completed. They sometimes land in spam, so check there too. I can have someone make sure the right ones are resent if you'd like."

HOW TO SUBMIT / FORMAT DOCUMENTS
"You can upload documents to our MyCase portal, email them, mail them, or drop them off at the Anoka office during business hours. It's best to send them all at once and as PDFs rather than phone photos. The iPhone Notes scanner or the Android Google Drive scanner work well, and naming files clearly (like 'Wells Fargo checking.pdf') helps a lot."

PAYMENT METHODS / WHO CAN PAY
"We accept cash, check, money order, and ACH bank transfer. Debit cards work but carry a three percent transaction fee. We can't accept your own personal credit card for bankruptcy services, but a family member or friend may pay on your behalf with any accepted method, including a credit card, which also carries a three percent fee. Just forward them the electronic invoice email."

SIGNING THE FLAT FEE / RETAINER AGREEMENT
"During or after your consultation we email you a link to sign the agreement electronically. If you'd rather not use email, we can provide a hard copy by mail or in person, and you can return it signed with your documents."

TAX DOCUMENTS NEEDED
"We'll need complete copies of your most recently filed federal and state returns, plus the prior year. If you don't have the returns, your W-2s or 1099s for those years can work, and if you didn't file, we can prepare a notarized affidavit for you."

INCOME DOCUMENTATION (paystubs, self-employment, benefits)
"It depends on your income type. For a job, your HR or online payroll portal has your paystubs. For self-employment, six months of business bank statements. For Social Security, retirement, disability, workers' comp, or VA, a current-year benefit letter or six months of bank statements works. For unemployment, you can pull your payment history at uimn.org."

VALUING YOUR PROPERTY / ASSETS
"Value items at what they'd realistically sell for in their current condition, like a retail consignment estimate, not garage-sale or pawn prices. For vehicles, use the private-party value from Kelly Blue Book, Edmunds, or NADA."

WHAT DEBTS DO I NEED TO LIST
"We pull your credit report, so you don't need account numbers or balances. Just let us know about any debts that might not show up on a credit report, like medical bills or personal loans."
